What is the marginal cost?
the marginal cost is a measurement of the incremental cost incurred from the production of each additional unit of a product.
How to calculate marginal cost?
To calculate marginal cost you should use the following formula:
marginal cost = change in costs / change in quantity
example: company x in it's first year produced 90 units a total costs of $17,000 and in the second year it produced 180 units with a total cost of $21,000, calculate the marginal cost.
using the marginal cost formula the answer will be:
marginal cost = change in costs / change in quantity
marginal cost = ($21,000 - $17,000) / (180 - 90)
marginal cost = $44.44
Why use the marginal cost?
the marginal cost is used to calculate at what point a company can achieve economies of scale to optimize production and overall operations.
